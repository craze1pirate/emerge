Paper Presentation

The Paper Presentation Event is a platform for curious minds to explore and share the latest research and innovations. It's a premier opportunity to present your findings, engage in scholarly discussions with peers and faculty, and gain new perspectives.

Event Guidelines

  • Round 1: Abstract Submission - Participants must submit their abstract and student details as a single PDF to the specified email address.
  • Round 2: Physical Presentation - Selected students must present their paper in person on September 20, 2025.

Rules

  • Participation can be solo or in a team of up to three members.
  • Papers should focus on topics related to EEE (e.g., IoT, Embedded Systems).
  • Each team member must register individually.
  • Presentation must be in MS-PowerPoint (16:9 layout, PPT format).
  • Abstracts must not exceed 150 words (Times New Roman, Headings 14, Content 12).
  • Presentation must not exceed 15 slides.
  • Submission deadline for abstract is September 20, 2025.
  • Maximum presentation time is 10 minutes.

Project Expo

The Project Expo is a platform for students to showcase their innovative projects and technical skills. This event fosters creativity and practical application of knowledge, providing a space for you to share your passion and receive valuable feedback.

Event Guidelines

  • Round 1: Abstract Submission - Send abstract, prototype photo, and student details as a PDF through the Google form.
  • Round 2: Demonstration & Presentation - Selected students must present their project in person on September 20, 2025.

Rules

  • Individual or team of maximum four members.
  • Project can be based on topics related to EEE and Emerging Topics.
  • Individual registration required for all team members.
  • Abstract should not exceed 150 words.
  • Time slot: 10 mins presentation followed by a 5 mins Q&A session.
  • Teams are responsible for bringing all necessary equipment.
  • Participants must bring their prototype.

Circuitrix

Circuitrix is a dynamic event designed to put your electrical and electronics engineering skills to the ultimate test. Engage in a series of fast-paced challenges that will assess your technical knowledge, quick thinking, and ability to collaborate under pressure.

Event Guidelines

  • Round 1: Bingo - Players mark electrical symbols on a pamphlet. First to get five in a line wins.
  • Round 2: Transmitter and Receiver - Teams of three communicate answers through acting, talking, or drawing.
  • Round 3: Simulation - One member memorizes a circuit, then explains it to teammates who must recreate it.

Rules

  • Participation is open to teams of up to three members.
  • All decisions made by the judges are final and binding.
  • Any form of cheating will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Participants must adhere to the time limits specified for each round.

Quizzical

Step into the electrifying world of Quizzical, an event designed to spark your mind and test your technical instincts. Packed with puzzles, visuals, and lightning-fast thinking, this contest blends knowledge, wit, and quick reflexes.

Event Guidelines

  • Round 1: Pictricity - Decode images representing components, topics, or personalities.
  • Round 2: Tech Charades - One teammate acts out a technical term for the others to guess.
  • Round 3: Rapid Relay - A lightning-fast, rapid-fire buzzer round.

Rules

  • Open to teams of up to three members.
  • The decision of the quizmaster is final.
  • Use of mobile phones or cheating will result in disqualification.
  • Teams must be on time for all rounds.

Treasure Hunt

Get ready for an electrifying adventure! This high-voltage treasure hunt is designed for the sharpest minds in EEE. Form a team of three and embark on a circuitous quest to test your technical knowledge, quick thinking, and collaborative skills.

Event Guidelines

  • Round 1: Current Affairs (MCQ Quiz) - A quiz based on concepts from electrical and electronic circuits.
  • Round 2: Watt Did You Say? - A lip-reading and gesture-based guessing game.
  • Round 3: Missing Phase Mystery - Teams solve a circuit puzzle with a missing component.

Rules

  • Each team must consist of three members.
  • The final decision is at the discretion of the event organizers.
  • Cheating or unsportsmanlike conduct will result in disqualification.
  • All team members must be present and work together.
  • No mobile phones or electronic gadgets allowed.
